Talking Tube

Talking Tube is a physics-based lumped two-dimensional (2D) vocal tract model having acoustic characteristics comparable to a precise three-dimensional (3D) vocal tract model. It improves the existing 2D vocal tracts to provide better geometrical flexibility at a lower computational overhead.

Introduction

Speech production is a complex but unique physiological process which requires the neuromuscular and biomechanical control of the human vocal apparatus (e.g., lips, jaw, tongue, etc.). A possible way of exploring some of these underlying speech production mechanisms is to build an articulatory-acoustic model, i.e., a quantitative computer-implemented emulation of the human speech organs that can generate acoustic speech signals (Kroger, 2022).
