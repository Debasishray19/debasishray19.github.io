2.5D Acoustics: Bridging Low-Dimensional Efficiency with 3D Accuracy
Debasish Ray Mohapatra, Victor Zappi, Sidney Fels

Introduction

The 2.5D propagation model is a physics-based acoustic wave solver that effectively approximates wave propagation within tube-like geometries using the finite-difference numerical scheme (FDTD). It seamlessly combines the computational efficiency characteristic of low-dimensional models with the accuracy of three-dimensional approaches. Unlike one- and two-dimensional models, which are confined to radial symmetry, our lightweight 2.5D solver adeptly accommodates irregular geometries, guided solely by mid-sagittal symmetry.

Below, we showcase the geometries of three virtual bores and present the corresponding audio samples synthesized using the 2.5D solver. The audio samples have a consistent pitch (approximately 261Hz) but different timbral signatures.
