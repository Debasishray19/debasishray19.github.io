2.5D Acoustics: Bridging Low-Dimensional Efficiency with 3D Accuracy
Debasish Ray Mohapatra, Victor Zappi, Sidney Fels

Introduction

The 2.5D acoustic wave solver is an expanded two-dimensional model that effectively approximates wave propagation in tube-like geometries through the finite-difference numerical scheme. It seamlessly combines the computational efficiency characteristic of low-dimensional models with the precision associated with three-dimensional methods. Unlike one- and two-dimensional models, which are confined to radial symmetry, our lightweight 2.5D solver adeptly accommodates irregular geometries, guided solely by mid-sagittal symmetry.

Below, we showcase the geometries of three virtual bores and present the corresponding audio samples synthesized using the 2.5D solver. The audio samples have a consistent pitch (approximately 261Hz) but different timbral signatures.
